# Lazy Otter Charters sample — image credits

This is a **cold-outreach sample** built by **BlueWave Projects**
(https://bluewaveprojects.com) at `/demos/lazy-otter-charters`. It is **NOT
affiliated with or endorsed by Lazy Otter Charters**. A real build would use
the operator's own official photography, branding, and logo.

All photos below are license-clean (public-domain or Creative-Commons) images
sourced from Wikimedia Commons. Each license was verified directly via the
Wikimedia Commons API (`imageinfo`/`extmetadata`) on 2026-07-03. None depict
Lazy Otter Charters' own boats, crew, guests, or copyrighted photography —
these are general Prince William Sound / Whittier scenery and wildlife used
as sample placeholders. Credits are also surfaced on-image and in the page's
honest sample note.

## Notes

- Originals were downloaded with `curl` (a descriptive User-Agent identifying
  this research, per Wikimedia API etiquette) and converted to optimized
  WebP (`cwebp` q82; hero resized to 2000px wide, all others to 1400–1600px
  wide). Source JPGs were removed after conversion.
- `whittier-passage-canal-sunrise.webp` and `whittier-harbor.webp` are both
  genuinely Whittier / Passage Canal, AK — the real gateway harbor Lazy Otter
  Charters operates from ("end of Harbor Rd — look for the green roof").
  Neither depicts Lazy Otter's own vessels.
- `kayakers-pws-whittier.webp` is geo-specific to Prince William Sound near
  Whittier, matching the operator's guided-kayaking offering, but shows
  generic kayakers, not Lazy Otter guests or guides.
- No identifiable individuals are used as featured subjects in a way that
  misrepresents them as Lazy Otter customers, staff, or guides.
- The final build for Lazy Otter Charters would use the operator's own
  official photography of its boats, crew, and Prince William Sound tours.
