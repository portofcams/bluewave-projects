# Kodiak Crab Festival sample site — image credits

This is an unaffiliated cold-outreach sample built by BlueWave Projects. All
photos below are license-clean (public domain or Creative-Commons) images
sourced from Wikimedia Commons and verified via the Commons API
(`imageinfo` / `extmetadata`). None are the Kodiak Crab Festival's own
copyrighted photography — the final build for the festival would use their
own official photography, branding, and logo. Credits are also surfaced
on-image and in the page's honest sample-disclaimer footer.

Notes:
- Originals were downloaded with curl (identifying UA + contact email) and
  converted to optimized WebP (`cwebp` q82; oversized originals downsampled
  with `sips` first — hero to 2000px wide, others to 1400–1600px on the long
  edge). Source JPGs were removed after conversion.
- The hero photo (USCG SAR demo) was taken **at the actual Kodiak Crab
  Festival** (May 28, 2011, St. Paul Harbor) — it is public-domain U.S.
  government work and is the one image on this page genuinely tied to the
  festival itself, not just Kodiak generally.
- "Blessing of the Fleet" is a real Kodiak Crab Festival tradition, but no
  license-clean photo specifically of Kodiak's own ceremony was found. The
  photo used depicts the same tradition (a fishing vessel blessing) at the
  Port of Juneau's own annual Blessing of the Fleet — used honestly as a
  generic stand-in for the ceremony type, not represented as Kodiak's event.
  This is disclosed on-page and here.
- The remaining images (fleet, harbor, fishing boat, crab pots, downtown) are
  genuine Kodiak, Alaska scenes — commercial fishing fleet, harbor, and town
  — used as general place/industry imagery for the community the festival
  celebrates, not photos of the festival itself.
- No fitting license-clean photo was found for the Grand Parade, the Norm
  Holm Survival Suit Race, or the carnival/inflatable attractions specifically
  — those sections keep designed SVG placeholder art with an honest "final
  build uses the festival's own photography" note.
